Overview

Certified & Uncertified Electricity

When electricity is generated, it can be certified with an Energy Attribute Certificate (EAC) — a digital proof of where and how each megawatt-hour was produced. This platform maps which electricity is certified, what remains uncertified, and the carbon footprint of each country's mix.

🏷️

Certified Mix

Electricity backed by verified Energy Attribute Certificates — such as Guarantees of Origin (GO) or I-RECs. Each certificate proves the source, location, and production period of a specific megawatt-hour of electricity.

⚡

Residual Mix

Electricity generation not claimed by any certificate. This is the "default" mix that uncertified consumers effectively receive — a blend of all electricity that wasn't specifically tracked. The lower the residual, the more transparent the market.

🌿

Emission Factor

The carbon intensity of electricity in a given country and year, expressed as tonnes of CO₂ per MWh. Calculated separately for the total generation mix and for the residual mix, enabling precise scope 2 emissions accounting.

Key Calculations

How Values Are Derived

Several metrics require multi-step calculations that differ by certificate framework. These are the core formulas applied during the pipeline — each approach reflects the data available per market.

AIB / GO Method

Residual Mix

ResidualMix =
  UntrackedFraction  × SupplierVolume  × SourceShare
UntrackedFraction Share of total supply not backed by cancelled GOs — sourced from the Residual Mix Table
SupplierVolume Total TWh supplied to the market, from the AIB Total Supplier Mix table
SourceShare This technology's fraction of total generation for the country & year
Uses official AIB market-wide figures, accounting for cross-border GO trades and cancellations. This authoritative value overrides the simpler arithmetic method wherever AIB data is available.
I-REC Method

Residual Mix

ResidualMix = max( 0 , TotalGeneration − CertifiedMix )
TotalGeneration Total electricity generated for this technology (TWh), from EMBER
CertifiedMix Total I-REC issuance for this technology, converted to TWh
Direct subtraction of certificate issuance from total generation. Clipped at zero — issuance occasionally exceeds metered generation for behind-the-meter installations not captured in national statistics.
Emission Factor

Carbon Intensity

EmissionFactor = Total CO₂ (ktCO₂) Total Generation (TWh) = tCO₂ / MWh
Total CO₂ Power sector CO₂ in ktCO₂ — from EMBER for I-REC countries; from AIB CO₂ intensity × generation for GO countries
Total Generation Total electricity generation in TWh for the country & year
Unit identity: ktCO₂ ÷ TWh = tCO₂ / MWh, equivalent to gCO₂ / kWh. For GO countries, the AIB-reported CO₂ intensity per technology replaces EMBER emissions to ensure consistency with the AIB production figures.
Certificate Selection

Certified Mix

CertifiedMix =
GO Issuance if GO data available for this country
All EAC Issuance otherwise (I-REC + TIGRS + LGC + Ecogox)
GO Issuance Net GO volume: certificates issued minus expired, from AIB/EECS data
All EAC Issuance Sum across all available registries: I-REC, TIGRS, Ecogox, and LGC
GO issuance is preferred where available — it reflects the European market's net-cancellation tracking, which precisely matches what consumers have claimed. For I-REC markets the sum of all registries is used as the certified total.
Methodology

Certificate Frameworks: GO & I-REC

Depending on the country, electricity tracking follows one of two established certificate frameworks. The platform automatically determines which applies per country based on total certificate volumes across all years.

🇪🇺

GO — Guarantee of Origin

Used across European and associated markets, managed through the AIB network. Each GO certifies exactly 1 MWh of electricity from a specific source and production period. GOs can be traded across borders, enabling companies anywhere in Europe to make specific renewable electricity claims.

  • Covers most European countries
  • Administered by AIB member bodies (EECS system)
  • Generation data: AIB Production Mix (authoritative)
  • Residual mix: calculated from official AIB Residual Mix reports
🌍

I-REC — International REC

Used in markets outside Europe — primarily Asia, Middle East, Africa, and Latin America. One I-REC certifies 1 MWh of verified renewable electricity. The I-REC Standard is internationally recognised and widely used for corporate scope 2 emissions accounting under the GHG Protocol.

  • Covers non-European markets
  • Issued under the I-REC Standard
  • Generation data: EMBER yearly dataset (primary)
  • Residual mix: derived from generation minus certificate issuance
